Security Fundamentals

  • Have I Been Pwned?
    Check if your personal email address or phone number has has been compromised and leaked. Have I Been Pwned is a website that allows Internet users to check whether their personal data has been compromised by data breaches. Visit HaveIBeenPWNED. If your data or passwords have been leaked, definitely change passwords, do not re-use previous ones and set up 2FA from point below.
  • 2FA for email & social media
    Set your two-factor authentication to all your important onlince accounts. Two-factor authentication (often shortened to 2FA) provides a way of 'double checking' that you really are the person you are claiming to be when you're using online services, such as banking, email or social media. Protect your email, LinkedIn, Facebook, shopping retaile accounts with 2FA/MFA. Doing this makes it harder for criminals to access your online accounts, even if they know your password. There are multiple ways authenticators out there, but you can check for example: Google Authenticator / Microsoft AUthentiacator or read this article and watch belove video Watch video to learn what is MFA: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AMOtB7XkTT4 Article guidance for personal 2FA setup: https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/guidance/setting-two-factor-authentication-2fa
  • Password managers
    Do not reuse passwords or create simple ones that easily come to your mind, you can random create difficult ones. A good password manager stores, generates, and updates passwords for you with the press of a button. You can get free or paid version, sync across all devices. and most importantly, you will only need to rememebr this one password. When you type that into the password manager, it unlocks the vault containing all of your actual passwords. Beware, only needing to remember one password is great, but it means there's a lot riding on that one password. Make sure it's a good one. Additional article to read: https://www.wired.com/story/best-password-managers/
  • Change default password on home Router and to WiFi
    Log into your router and change its default login, then do the same for your wifi password at home Default passwords can be widely known and hackers can get into your personal network in seconds. Once you connect to your router and change password, there are multiple other things you could do: change network name, change octet values, see and block connected devices, disable used ports, etc - but that would be covered in advanced section afterwards. Additional reading in regards to default passwords, which is always a bad thing: https://www.lifewire.com/changing-default-password-on-wifi-network-816567
  • Get an Antivirus
    There are multiple providers and it would be primarly your own choice, but it is always good to have and updated antivirus solution for added protection.
  • Set Firewall
    To be sure your computer is clean, try checking yourself and your closest with this free Microsoft scanner scan tool is designed to find and remove malware from Windows computers. Simply download it and run a scan to find malware and try to reverse changes made by identified threats. For Windows users, you could try this one: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/security/threat-protection/intelligence/safety-scanner-download
